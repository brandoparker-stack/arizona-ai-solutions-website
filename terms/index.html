6. SMS Messaging

6.1 Consent and Opt-In

  • No cold outreach: SMS messages are sent only in response to a customer's inbound phone call. Arizona AI Solutions does not send unsolicited, promotional, or marketing text messages under any circumstances.
  • Verbal consent required: During each inbound call, the AI agent asks the customer for clear, affirmative consent before sending any SMS. Example: "Would you like me to send your booking confirmation via text message?" The customer must verbally confirm (e.g., "Yes," "Sure," "Go ahead") before a message is sent. If the customer declines or does not respond, no SMS is sent.
  • Scope of consent: Consent applies only to transactional messages related to the specific service interaction discussed on that call — booking confirmations, appointment reminders, reschedule links, follow-ups, and review requests. Consent does not extend to promotional offers, newsletters, seasonal discounts, marketing campaigns, or any messages unrelated to that appointment. Consent never authorizes marketing or promotional messages under any circumstances.
  • Per-call consent: Each new inbound call requires fresh verbal consent before any new SMS messages are sent. Prior consent from a previous call does not carry over to new calls or new appointments.
  • Consent verification: Arizona AI Solutions maintains a record of each consent event, including the timestamp, call identifier, and message type triggered. This log is available for carrier compliance audits upon request.

6.2 Message Types and Frequency

  • Booking confirmations — Sent immediately after an appointment is booked on the call.
  • Appointment reminders — Sent approximately 24 hours before a scheduled appointment.
  • Reschedule links — Sent when a customer requests to change an appointment time.
  • Post-appointment follow-ups — Sent approximately 1 hour after a completed appointment, including a review request link.
  • Emergency notifications — Sent to the business owner when a caller reports an emergency (e.g., burst pipe, gas leak, electrical hazard).

Customers can expect 1 to 3 SMS messages per appointment (confirmation, reminder, follow-up). This is an event-triggered, transactional message pattern — not recurring or subscription-based messaging.

6.3 Opt-Out Mechanics

  • STOP: Immediately cancels all future SMS messages. After sending STOP, the customer receives a single confirmation message stating they have been unsubscribed. No further messages will be sent unless the customer re-opts in.
  • UNSTOP: Restores SMS delivery. The customer receives a confirmation message that messaging has been resumed. Opt-in consent is reaffirmed at this point.
  • HELP: Replies with a help message containing support contact information and a reminder that the customer can reply STOP to unsubscribe.
  • Processing time: Opt-out requests are processed immediately upon receipt. There is no delay between receiving a STOP command and halting message delivery.
  • Post opt-out: After a customer opts out via STOP, no further SMS messages will be sent to that number unless the customer calls the business again and provides fresh verbal consent, or sends UNSTOP to resume.
